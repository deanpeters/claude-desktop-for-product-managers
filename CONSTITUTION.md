# CONSTITUTION.md

## Purpose

`claude-desktop-for-product-managers` exists to help Product Managers work effectively with Claude Desktop in real product workflows.

The opinion this course holds: **Claude is most valuable when it helps a PM reason through real artifacts and make better decisions — not when it performs impressive-looking demos.**

## What this project is

- an interactive course delivered through Claude Desktop Cowork sessions
- a library of realistic PM exercises against a fictional practice company
- a reference layer for fast lookup and reinforcement

## What this project is not

- a derivative rebrand of anyone else's course
- a prompt collection
- a coding bootcamp in disguise
- an argument for replacing Product Managers with AI

## Clean-room rule

Everything here is written from scratch.

- no copied or lightly paraphrased lesson text from other courses
- no reused fictional company — the practice company here is distinct from every adjacent course's sandbox
- no replicated teaching scripts or brand language
- structural inspiration from siblings is acknowledged; their words are never borrowed

## Client-IP boundary

No real company name — client, prospect, competitor, or vendor — appears in this repo, including inside these guardrail docs. Exercises and examples are laundered into fully generic, fictional form. When material derived from real engagements is generalized, it must be unrecognizable: different industry surface, different scale, different names, different numbers. Uncertain? Ask Dean before writing it down.

## Audience

Primary: Product Managers, product leads, consultants, and trainers.
Secondary: founders doing product work, PM-enablement engineers, students of modern product practice.

Assume zero terminal fluency. Assume high product fluency.

## Teaching principles

1. Learn by doing, on files that feel real.
2. Keep human judgment visible — every lesson names where the PM must think.
3. Show failure modes and review points, not just happy paths.
4. Prefer repeatable workflows over clever one-off prompts.
5. PRD last: artifacts come after the thinking has improved.

## Definition of done

A lesson is done when a Product Manager can open the files, understand the task, ask Claude useful things, judge the result against the checklist, and adapt the workflow to their own product — without help.
