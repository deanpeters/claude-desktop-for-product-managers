Making your practice repeatable: first for you, then for your team.

## Why and when

A good week with Claude is nice. A repeatable week is a career change. This module turns your wins into workflows: durable context, packaged skills, connected tools, and longer-running work you supervise instead of babysit. You'll reach for it the second time you catch yourself doing the same task from scratch, and the day a teammate asks how you're suddenly this fast.

## What you'll cover

- Projects and memory: durable context so Claude stops starting from zero
- Skills: packaging a workflow so it runs the same way twice
- connectors: reaching the tools your team already lives in
- loops and goals: giving Claude an outcome and a cadence, with checkpoints you set
- quality-control patterns and review discipline
- packaging your workflow into something colleagues can adopt

## Why this comes before launch

Module 6 assumes this toolkit exists. Launch reporting, repeatable launch assets, pushing work into team tools: all of it leans on what you build here.

## Where the learning happens

Not on this page. This site is your map; the classroom is Claude Desktop. Open a Cowork session pointed at the `course-materials` folder and say: *"Take me to Module 5."*

If you're lost mid-module, paste this into that session, even a brand-new one:

```text
I'm taking the Claude Desktop for Product Managers course and I'm lost
somewhere in Module 5 (Advanced workflows). Read COMPANION_INSTRUCTIONS.md
and my-progress.md in this folder, tell me where I am, and offer me
three ways forward.
```
