**Learn to do your actual job with AI, by doing your actual job with AI.**

This is a hands-on course for product managers and product leaders. You'll use Claude Desktop the way you'd work with a sharp colleague: synthesizing discovery, framing problems, testing risky ideas cheaply, producing delivery artifacts, and taking a product to market. Built by experienced product managers and educators. No hype, no code, no terminal.

## Who this is for

- PMs who keep hearing "just use AI" and want something more useful than a list of prompts
- Product leaders who want their teams working this way, credibly and consistently
- Consultants, founders, and aspiring PMs who do product work without the title

Any level of product experience works. Zero Claude Desktop experience is assumed.

## Why it works

- You learn on realistic product work, not toy demos
- One continuous story: you carry the same product from raw discovery all the way to launch
- Every lesson ends with a review checklist, so you always know whether your work passed
- Claude teaches, checks your work honestly, and hands every judgment call back to you

## How to start

1. Download the course folder and unzip it. No git, no installs beyond Claude Desktop itself.
2. Open Claude Desktop and point a Cowork session at `course-materials/`.
3. Say: *"I'm starting the course. Read COMPANION_INSTRUCTIONS.md and take me to Module 0."*

This site is the quick-reference layer: recaps, lookups, and the map of where you are. The folder is the course.

## The arc

Seven modules, one product story:

1. **Getting Set Up**: Claude Desktop in plain PM English, and how the course works
2. **Fundamentals**: read, synthesize, structure, edit, verify
3. **Problem Space**: discovery synthesis, segments, personas, JTBD, framing
4. **Shaping the Opportunity**: name the bet, build the minimum viable narrative, prototype with Artifacts, pressure-test, decide
5. **Delivery Artifacts**: PoC slice, epic, stories, PRD last
6. **Advanced Workflows**: Projects, Skills, connectors, loops and goals
7. **Taking It to Market**: messaging, demo, GTM, rollout, automated reporting

## The operating principle

Confidence isn't evidence. Every Claude Desktop capability appears at the moment the product story needs it, and every concept you shape ends in a real decision: go, no-go, or revise and retest. No maybe.
